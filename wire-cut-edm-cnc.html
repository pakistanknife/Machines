About this Wire Cut EDM CNC

A Wire Cut EDM (Electrical Discharge Machine) uses a thin, electrically-charged brass or coated copper wire — submerged in a dielectric fluid — to cut precise profiles in any electrically conductive material. Unlike conventional milling or grinding, EDM removes material through controlled electrical sparks, with no mechanical contact between wire and workpiece. This makes it the only viable cutting method for hardened tool steels, tungsten carbide inserts, titanium alloys, and exotic metals where traditional machining is too slow or impossible.

The CNC (Computer Numerical Control) system on this machine handles complex 2D contours, tapered cuts, and tight-tolerance multi-pass strategies automatically. Typical achievable accuracy is ±0.005 mm with surface finish suitable for die work without secondary lapping. The CNC also enables unmanned overnight operation — a major productivity gain in tool-and-die shops, surgical instrument plants, and aerospace job shops.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions buyers ask about the Wire Cut EDM CNC.

What materials can a Wire Cut EDM cut?

Any electrically conductive material — hardened tool steels (D2, A2, M2, M4, etc.), tungsten carbide, titanium and titanium alloys, copper, brass, aluminum, Inconel, Hastelloy, and most stainless steels. Non-conductive materials (ceramics, plastics, glass) cannot be cut by EDM.

What is the typical cutting accuracy?

A well-maintained Wire EDM CNC achieves ±0.005 mm (5 microns) on the first cut, with multi-pass strategies tightening to ±0.002 mm. Surface finish is usually Ra 0.8 µm or better, often eliminating the need for secondary lapping.
