About this Big Capacity Vibrator

A vibratory finisher (also called vibratory tumbler, deburring machine, or bowl finisher) uses a vibrating bed of media — ceramic, plastic, steel, or porcelain shapes — to mass-finish metal parts. The action removes burrs, polishes surfaces, breaks edges, and prepares parts for plating or final finishing. Big-capacity bowls like this one are designed for production use, processing hundreds or thousands of small parts per cycle without operator attendance.

This is the workhorse of Sialkot’s surgical instrument and cutlery industry — practically every plant uses one or several. The big bowl reduces the number of cycles needed for a given batch, increasing throughput significantly. The machine handles both wet finishing (water + compound for general deburring and cleaning) and dry finishing (corn-cob or walnut shell for final polish and rust prevention).

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions buyers ask about the Big Capacity Vibrator.

What media should I use for surgical instruments?

Ceramic media in pyramid or angle-cut shapes is the most common for surgical work — it deburrs and pre-polishes simultaneously. A final mirror polish usually requires a dry corn-cob or walnut-shell pass with a polishing compound.

Wet or dry finishing?

Wet processing (water + compound) is most common for general deburring — it keeps parts cool and cleans simultaneously. Dry processing is used for final polish and rust prevention. The machine handles both.

How many parts per cycle?

Depends on part size and load ratio (typically 1:3 parts to media). For small surgical and cutlery parts, expect several hundred to several thousand per cycle.

What about maintenance?

Springs, motor bearings, and bowl liner are the main wear items. All commonly available locally in Sialkot.
