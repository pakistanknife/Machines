About this Vertical Milling Machine

A vertical milling machine has its spindle perpendicular to the work table, making it the most versatile single machine in a modern machine shop. From face milling to slot milling, drilling to boring, and pocket profiling to end-mill contouring, a vertical mill handles the bulk of general machining work that would otherwise require multiple specialized machines. The tilting head on most vertical mills also enables angled milling without complex fixturing.

For tool & die makers, mold builders, prototype shops, surgical instrument manufacturers, and small-batch production, the vertical mill is the daily driver. Combined with quality tooling (collets, vises, rotary table) it can perform 80% of the work needed for typical tool-room jobs. Used machines from a maintained shop hold their accuracy well — way ground surfaces and quality bearings often last many decades.
